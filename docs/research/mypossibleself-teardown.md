## A. Onboarding

### A1 — Schermata obiettivi ("I want to…")
Mascotte (faccia azzurra sorridente) + paesaggio. Bolla chat:

> Hello! It's great to meet you. I'm here to support you on your journey towards better mental health.
>
> What brings you to My Possible Self? We'll personalise your recommendations based on your goals.

**I want to…** (griglia di scelte, multi-selezione):
- Sleep better
- Manage anxiety
- Improve my mood
- Feel calm and relaxed
- Reduce stress
- Nurture, grow and heal
- Maintain a healthy body and mind
- Build habits, enhance relationships

Bottoni in fondo: **SOMETHING ELSE** · **CONTINUE**

### A2 — Registrazione ("Nearly there…")
- **What would you like us to call you?** → campo nome (es. "sajid") con spunta verde
- **Are you signing up as part of an organisation?** → No / Yes
- Blocco privacy: *"Data protection laws allow you to control how your information is processed and stored. **Your rights explained.**"*
  - ☐ I understand my data protection rights and consent to my data being stored.
  - ☐ I am 18 or older and agree to the **terms and conditions**
  - ☐ I am happy to receive news and offers from My Possible Self via email.
- Bottone arancione: **Continue**
- Link: *Already have an account? Sign in >* · *FAQs >* · *Terms and conditions >*

> 📌 Nota BAB: qui c'è già il pattern "nome (anche di fantasia) + checkbox consenso + flag 18+". Utile per il nostro onboarding pseudonimo.

### B4 — Sintomi fisici (carosello a categorie)
> Choose which physical symptoms you felt

Tre categorie scrollabili orizzontalmente, ognuna con card a scorrimento. Ogni card ha titolo, spiegazione fisiologica, icona, e due bottoni **✕ (no) / ✓ (sì)**.

**HEAD**
| Card | Testo |
|---|---|
| Brain hijacked | Your thoughts race so it's hard to think clearly or rationally. You may feel detached or as if things are 'unreal.' |
| Head dizzy | You may feel dizzy or light-headed. This is the result of faster breathing. |
| Mouth dries | The blood vessels in your mouth area narrow making your mouth feel dry. |
| Eyes widen | Your pupils may dilate to allow more light in. This can either improve or blur your vision. |

**BODY**
| Card | Testo |
|---|---|
| Breathe fast and shallow | This helps you take in more oxygen and you may experience a choking feeling. |
| Body heats and sweats | As your system speeds up your body will get hotter. Sweating helps you cool down. |
| Stomach churns | Adrenalin reduces your blood flow relaxing your stomach and intestinal muscles. This can cause nausea, 'butterflies' or churning. |
| Bladder relaxes | Your inner sphincter muscle relaxes so you might feel the urge to pass urine. But you can normally still control your outer sphincter and remain dry. |
| Heart beats faster | This pumps more blood to your muscles (so you're ready to run or attack). Your blood pressure and pulse increase and you may get palpitations. |

**LIMBS**
| Card | Testo |
|---|---|
| Muscles tense | Your blood, full of oxygen and glucose energy, is sent to your arm and leg muscles (so you're ready to fight or escape). It can cause aches and pains. |
| Hands tingle | Your small blood vessels may constrict directing blood to your larger muscles. This can cause tingling or numbness. |
| Legs tremble | You may get 'jelly legs' as your blood flows to into larger muscles. Trembling or numbness is caused by your small blood vessels constricting. |

### B6 — "Origins" (carosello educativo, ~13 slide)
Icona puzzle a 4 pezzi (che si compone slide dopo slide). Header fisso:
> **Origins** — It can be helpful to take a moment to consider where our beliefs have originated from – working with the idea we weren't born with them.

Slide (testo nella card centrale):
1. Our beliefs can be described as 'filters' to our perceptions of ourselves and our world
2. They are influenced by our environment, our relationships, our education, our experiences, our culture
3. They can be formed from jumping to conclusions about outcomes and reasons things happen
4. Beliefs are very much intertwined with our self-concept and sense of self (known as core beliefs)
5. They are commonly learnt from an early age from parents and other key authority figures
6. They can be learnt by accepting what others tell us is 'true'; and we then honour other people's beliefs
7. They can also be influenced and reinforced by the media, and any other external information we are exposed to
8. Many limiting beliefs can come from our childhood when we haven't always been able to think for ourselves
9. Challenging or stressful experiences can also lead to unhelpful belief systems
10. When we change our thinking, we change our beliefs
11. When we change our beliefs, we change our behaviour

## Pattern UX riutilizzabili per BAB (sintesi)

1. **Mascotte + tono conversazionale** che "parla" all'utente (bolle chat in onboarding).
2. **Onboarding per obiettivi** a griglia → personalizza i contenuti successivi.
3. **Micro-interazioni semplici**: slider Low→High, swipe di card ✕/✓, tile a selezione multipla. Pochissima digitazione (un solo textarea, opzionale, 0/300).
4. **Caroselli educativi brevi** (una frase per schermata) per psicoeducazione → adattabili a contenuti su ciclo/energia/RED-S.
5. **"Escape hatch" sempre visibile** (box breathing in footer): in ogni momento puoi accedere a uno strumento di auto-regolazione.
6. **Spiegazione fisiologica de-drammatizzante** ("questo è normale, ecco perché succede") — trasferibile a fatica/umore/ciclo nello sport.
7. **Consenso e flag età integrati nell'onboarding**, non in un blocco legale separato.

## Cosa NON trasferiamo così com'è
- È un **modulo CBT clinico** (ansia, credenze limitanti), non un diario quotidiano. Per BAB serve invertire la frequenza: **check-in giornaliero breve** > modulo lungo occasionale.
- Le liste di credenze negative ("I am worthless"…) sono pesanti e cliniche: per un test su **atlete giovani** vanno riformulate e alleggerite, con eventuale supervisione di un esperto clinico.
