For too long, women's sport has been coached as if it were a smaller version of men's sport. But a teenage athlete's body changes — and with it, energy, recovery and mood shift across the menstrual cycle.

## A number worth sitting with

In a study of 128 elite athletes, **77% report that their cycle affects performance** (Jones & Bishop, 2024). It isn't a feeling: it's a signal from the body that deserves attention, not shame.

Yet research on the topic is almost non-existent: only **6% of sports-science studies** are conducted on women only (Cowley et al., 2021, *Invisible Sportswomen*).

## What an athlete can do, starting today

- **Observe, don't judge.** Logging energy, mood and sleep day by day helps reveal your own patterns.
- **Adapt, don't stop.** Knowing the phases lets you dose load and recovery instead of being caught off guard.
- **Talk about it.** A shared language with coaches and family turns a taboo into an advantage.
